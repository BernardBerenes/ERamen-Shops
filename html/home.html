Kurume Ramen

The noodles in Kurume ramen are thin and straight, usually made from wheat flour. They have a firm and chewy texture, which pairs well with the rich broth. The ramen is often topped with various ingredients such as slices of tender chashu (braised pork), marinated bamboo shoots, scallions, and sometimes a soft-boiled egg. Some variations may include additional toppings like seaweed, bean sprouts, or black garlic oil.

Hakata Ramen

Hakata ramen, originating from Fukuoka, Japan, features a rich tonkotsu broth made from simmered pork bones. Thin and firm noodles provide a satisfying chewiness. Toppings like chashu pork, pickled ginger, menma bamboo shoots, green onions, and sesame seeds complete the dish. It's a simple yet flavorful bowl of ramen beloved by enthusiasts worldwide.
